Get Involved

Participate in the sharing economy and help create a more sustainable campus community. The simplest way to get involved is to use the Lending Library!

The Impact of Our Sharing Economy

By borrowing instead of buying, the U-M community reduces waste, saves resources, and builds a culture of sustainability. Here's how our lending library makes a difference:

♻️

Reduced Waste

500+ pounds of waste diverted annually

Every item borrowed is one less item purchased and eventually discarded. Our lending library helps keep perfectly usable materials out of landfills.

💰

Cost Savings

$10,000+ saved by student groups

Student organizations and campus departments save money by borrowing instead of purchasing equipment they'll only use occasionally.

🌱

Resource Conservation

30% reduction in new purchases

Sharing reduces resource extraction, manufacturing impacts, and carbon emissions associated with producing new items.

🤝

Community Building

200+ groups served annually

The lending library strengthens campus connections and fosters cooperation across organizations and departments.

How to Participate

Getting involved with the Sustainability Lending Library is simple! The most effective way to participate is to use the service and spread the word.

1

Borrow Equipment

The most direct way to participate is to borrow items for your events and projects instead of purchasing new ones. Each borrowing action reduces waste and resource consumption.

2

Spread the Word

Tell your friends, classmates, and other campus organizations about the Lending Library. The more people who use it, the greater our collective impact!

3

Plan Sustainable Events

Use our resources to make your events more sustainable. From reusable dishware to energy-efficient equipment, we can help reduce the environmental footprint of your activities.

4

Share Your Impact

When you use our equipment, share photos on social media using #UMSustainabilityLending to showcase how you're contributing to campus sustainability.

Ready to make a difference?

Start borrowing today and be part of the campus sharing economy movement!
